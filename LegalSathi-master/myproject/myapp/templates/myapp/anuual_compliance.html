FAQs about Annual Compliance LLP

Q1: What is the deadline for filing the Annual Return for LLP in India?

The Annual Return for LLP in India must be filed within 60 days from the end of the financial year.

Q2: Is it mandatory to appoint an auditor for an LLP?

Yes, it is mandatory to appoint an auditor if the LLP's turnover exceeds the prescribed limit.

Q3: What happens if an LLP fails to comply with annual compliance requirements?

Failure to comply with annual compliance can result in penalties, legal actions, and loss of good standing with regulatory authorities.

Q4: Are LLPs required to hold an Annual General Meeting (AGM)?

While LLPs are not required to hold an AGM, they must still comply with statutory meetings as per the LLP agreement.

Q5: Can LLPs outsource their compliance obligations to a service provider?

Yes, LLPs can outsource compliance obligations to a qualified service provider to ensure timely and accurate filings.

Q6: Are LLPs required to maintain proper books of accounts?

Yes, LLPs must maintain proper books of accounts and other relevant documents at their registered office address.

Q7: What tax compliance requirements do LLPs need to fulfil?

LLPs need to file income tax returns, GST returns, and other applicable tax filings to remain compliant.

Q8: Can annual compliance for LLPs be completed online?

Yes, many aspects of annual compliance for LLPs can be completed online, including filing of returns and forms.

Q9: What are the consequences of not appointing an auditor for an LLP?

Not appointing an auditor when required can result in penalties and non-compliance with statutory obligations.

Q10: How often should LLPs review their compliance status?

LLPs should review their compliance status annually to ensure they meet all legal and regulatory requirements.
