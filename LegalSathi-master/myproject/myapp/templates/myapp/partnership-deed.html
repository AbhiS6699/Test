What is a Partnership Deed?

A partnership deed is an agreement between the partners containing a Partnership agreement between the partners of the firm. The deed includes the terms and conditions of the partnership like the nature of the business, a specific state in which the firm will operate its business activities, partnership firm structure, or a clause related to the dissolution of the firm under the “Indian Partnership Act of 1932”.

Documents Required for Partnership Deed Drafting

Identity and Address Proof of Partners

PAN Cards of Partners

Passport-sized Photographs of Partners

Proof of Office Address from where the business will be operated

Utility Bill of Registered Office Address like electricity bill

No Objection Certificate (NOC) from the Property Owner (if applicable)

Rent Agreement or registry papers of the property from where the business will be operated

Stamp Paper for Partnership Deed Notarization

Validity of Partnership Registration in India

  • A registered partnership firm can continue its operations up to the wish of the partners
  • Flexible Changes in Partnership Agreement
  • Filing of proper Annual Returns even though it is not mandatory
  • The registration becomes invalid upon the dissolution or termination of the partnership
  • Partners must comply with applicable laws and regulations governing partnerships
  • It is crucial to follow the legal procedures for the conversion of a partnership into another business structure

Advantages of a Partnership Firm

  • Subject to Fewer Regulations
  • One can apply for registration without any strict formalities associated with other business structures.
  • Flexibility in Decision-Making
  • Just like a registered firm, an unregistered firm cannot enjoy certain benefits and legal protections.
  • No Mandatory Annual Filings
  • No Minimum Capital Requirement
  • Unlike a Limited Liability Partnership (LLP), a partnership firm is not mandated to obtain a certificate of registration.
  • Conversion of type of firm is easy
  • A unique identification number or registration certificate is not mandatory for a partnership firm.
  • The registration process for a partnership firm in India is relatively less lengthy.

Documents and Details Required for Registration

  • Partnership Deed
  • Form 1 - Application for registration which is available online
  • PAN cards of the partners
  • Aadhar Cards of the partners
  • Address proofs of the partners
  • Address Proof of Office Address (electricity bill, rent agreement, etc.)
  • Affidavit from partners per the Partnership Act
  • The firm's current account details along with other relevant bank documents
