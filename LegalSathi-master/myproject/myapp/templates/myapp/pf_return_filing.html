What is Online PF Return Filing?

PF returns are a crucial aspect of financial management for both employers and employees. PF return refers to the process of filing returns related to the Provident Fund (PF), a social security scheme provided by the government for employees in India. Under this scheme, both employers and employees make regular contributions towards the employee's retirement savings.

The PF return filing process is mandatory for all organizations covered under the PF scheme. It ensures compliance with regulations and helps maintain transparency in financial transactions related to employee benefits. Failure to file PF returns on time or accurately can lead to penalties and legal consequences for the employer.

What is the due date for filing PF return?

Private Establishments

  • PF returns must be submitted monthly.
  • The deadline for monthly submissions is the 15th of the following month.

Government Establishments

  • PF returns must be submitted quarterly.
  • The deadline for quarterly submissions is the end of the month following the quarter's conclusion.

Quick Overview

  • PF Payment: Due on or before the 15th of each month.
  • PF Annual Return: Due by April 25th of every year.

Annual PF return filing process

Annual PF returns must be filed no later than April 30th of each year. The forms required for filing annual PF returns are Form 3A and Form 6A.

Form 3A

Provides a detailed breakdown of monthly contributions made by both employees and employers to the Employee Provident Fund and the Employee Pension Fund throughout the year. It includes account number, subscriber's name, and establishment's details.

Form 6A

Serves as a consolidated annual contribution statement, summarizing the contributions made by each member of the establishment for the entire year.
