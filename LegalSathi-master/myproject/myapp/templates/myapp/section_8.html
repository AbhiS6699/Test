FAQs about Section 8 Company

Q1. What is a Section 8 Company?

A Section 8 Company, as per the Companies Act, 2013 in India, is a non-profit organisation formed with the objective of promoting social welfare, charity, or any other useful purpose. These companies are prohibited from distributing dividends to their members and instead utilise their income for promoting their stated objectives.

Q2. What are the minimum requirements to register a Section 8 Company?

The key requirements include a minimum of two directors and the submission of required documents for registration.

Q3. Can a Section 8 Company earn profits?

While a Section 8 Company can earn a surplus, it must be used solely for promoting the company’s objectives and cannot be distributed as dividends.

Q4. Can foreign nationals or Non-Resident Indians (NRIs) be directors or shareholders of a Section 8 Company?

Yes, foreign nationals and NRIs can be directors or shareholders, provided they fulfill the necessary requirements.

Q5. How long does it take to register a Section 8 Company?

The registration process typically takes 2 weeks, depending on the completion of required documentation and approvals.

Q6. What is the procedure for changing the registered office address of a Section 8 Company?

To change the registered office address, the Section 8 Company must follow the prescribed procedure, which includes obtaining board and shareholder approval, notifying the Registrar of Companies (RoC), and updating the necessary documents and records.
